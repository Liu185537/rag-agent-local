# Enrollment Messaging Playbook

## Handling price objections

When a parent says the class fee feels expensive, avoid defensive wording.
First acknowledge the concern, then connect price to concrete value outcomes.
Offer a trial class and summarize what progress can be measured in 2-4 weeks.
If budget is tight, provide phased payment options and a clear next step.

## Follow-up cadence

If a lead says they are busy, use a light cadence:
1. Follow up after 48 hours with one useful takeaway from the consultation.
2. Follow up again in 4 days with a short case example.
3. Follow up in 7 days with a clear yes/no decision prompt.
Keep each message under 4 lines and include one CTA.

## Qualification without pressure

Use open questions:
- "What outcome do you most want in the next 3 months?"
- "What made previous attempts hard to continue?"
- "How much weekly study time is realistic?"
Mirror the parent's wording and confirm understanding before proposing solutions.
Empathy first, recommendation second.
